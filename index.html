The Consequences of Inbreeding and Extreme Breeding Practices

Inbreeding in pets has led to an increase in inherited diseases. Breeding for extreme appearances often results in health issues that impact their quality of life.

What is the Current Problem Check-a-Pet is Trying to Solve?

Years of inbreeding and breeding for appearance have caused high rates of inherited diseases in purebred dogs. Breeding for extreme looks has led to painful conditions such as joint pain, breathing issues, and skin infections.

What Are the Issues?

  • Genetic Health Risks: Some breeds suffer from severe genetic diseases.
  • Breeding Challenges: Limited gene pools make breeding more complex and costly.
  • Cost of Health Testing: While health tests are expensive, they prevent costly vet visits later.
  • Outcrossing & Legal Responsibility: Breeders are legally responsible for the health of the dogs they breed.
